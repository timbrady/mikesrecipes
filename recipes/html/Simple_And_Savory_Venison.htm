SIMPLE AND SAVORY VENISON

 

Try this simple-but-savory venison tenderloin recipe from Executive Chef David Carrier from The Cloister at Sea Island resort in coastal Georgia.

By Alexis Korman

If you crave steak and lamb, but balk at venison, get ready to fall in love with this flavorful, healthy lean meat. Step one; score some fresh venison from your butcher. Step two; steal this simple-but-savory venison tenderloin recipe from Executive Chef David Carrier from The Cloister at Sea Island resort in coastal Georgia.

 

Venison Back Strap Medallions with Warm Roasted Fennel & Sweet Potato Salad

INGREDIENTS:

1. 4–6 tablespoons canola or grape seed oil

2. Salt and pepper

3. 2 3-inch medallions of venison loin

4. 2 tablespoons sorghum

5. 2 medium sweet potatoes

6. 1 fennel bulb

7. 4 ounces toasted walnut oil, divided

8. 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard

9. 1½ ounces Sherry vinegar

10. 1 teaspoon minced shallot

11. ½ cup toasted walnut halves

12. 1 teaspoon chopped parsley

13. 1 teaspoon chopped tarragon

DIRECTIONS:

Preheat a cast iron skillet until a drop of water dances on the bottom. Add enough canola or grape seed oil to coat the bottom of the pan. Salt and pepper the venison medallions and place in the preheated skillet. Cook venison on both sides until medium rare (130ûF), about 8 minuteÕs total. Remove the venison from the skillet. Before serving, brush with sorghum and place in 350ûF oven for three minutes.

For the salad, prick sweet potatoes with a fork, and place in a salted pie tin and cook at 350ûF until tender, about an hour. Pull off the skin while still hot and cut into 1-inch chunks. Cut the fennel bulb into 1-inch slices, toss with 1 tablespoon of walnut oil, a pinch of salt and pepper and roast in a pan next to the sweet potatoes until the fennel is tender, about 10 minutes. In a mixing bowl combine the mustard, vinegar and shallots, then whisk in the remaining walnut oil to form vinaigrette. Place the walnuts, potatoes and fennel into the mixing bowl and toss with the vinaigrette to coat. Then, season to taste with salt, parsley and tarragon. Serves 2.
