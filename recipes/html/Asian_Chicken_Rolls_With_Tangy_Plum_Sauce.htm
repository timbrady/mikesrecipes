ASIAN CHICKEN ROLLS WITH TANGY PLUM SAUCE

 

Make the rolls up to a day ahead, and then bake just before serving. The sauce can be made ahead too, but stir in the cilantro when you serve it or it will lose its vibrant color. Don’t let their small stature fool you. These little party “flavors” pack a tasty punch! Their best feature just might be their make-ahead qualities.

Makes 30-32 rolls, ¾ cup sauce

(*) Spread chicken mixture along the bottom edge of each piece of pastry, then roll to enclose. 

 

INGREDIENTS:

FOR THE ROLLS:

  1. 1/3 cup chopped scallions
  2. 6 Tbs. chopped fresh cilantro, divided
  3. ½ Tsp. ground ginger
  4. 1 lb. boneless, skinless chicken breast, cubed
  5. 2 Tbs. low-sodium soy sauce
  6. 1 Tsp. chili garlic sauce
  7. 1 egg, separated
  8. ½ cup canned water chestnuts, chopped
  9. 1 sheet purchased puff pastry
  10. 1 Tsp. each white and black sesame seeds

 

FOR THE SAUCE:

  1. ½ cup plum jam
  2. 2 Tbs. lime juice
  3. 2 Tbs. water
  4. 1 Tbs. ketchup
  5. ¼ Tsp. each salt and red pepper flakes

 

DIRECTIONS:

Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Then mince scallions, 3 Tbs. cilantro, and ginger in a food processor. Add chicken, soy sauce, chili garlic sauce, and egg white; pulse until coarsely chopped. Transfer to a bowl and fold in water chestnuts.

Roll pastry on a floured surface to 14-inch square, then halve lengthwise. Divide chicken mixture between portions of dough, spreading along the bottom edge of each piece of pastry. Roll to enclose filling and transfer to prepared baking sheet, seam side down. Beat yolk with 1 Tbs. water, brush onto logs, then sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake until golden, 20-25 minutes, let cool slightly, and then slice each log into 1-inch pieces.

 

Simmer all sauce ingredients in a saucepan over medium for 5 minutes. Let cool, then stir in remaining cilantro.
